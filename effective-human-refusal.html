LUMINA-30 core concept

Effective Human Refusal

In LUMINA-30, effective human refusal means that an affected human, authorized representative, responsible operator, or legitimate oversight body can meaningfully withhold, stop, suspend, or challenge a system before irreversible consequences occur.

Definition

In LUMINA-30, effective human refusal means that an affected human, authorized representative, responsible operator, or legitimate oversight body can meaningfully withhold, stop, suspend, or challenge a system before irreversible consequences occur.

General concept vs. LUMINA-30 usage

Human oversight is a general governance term and can include monitoring or approval. LUMINA-30 uses a narrower test: formal oversight is not enough if refusal lacks the information, time, authority, and operational effect needed to change the outcome.

Why this distinction matters

A nominal stop button, review committee, or approval step does not demonstrate effective refusal by itself. The refusal must still work at the relevant time and scope.

Limits and unknowns

The framework does not assign legal authority or decide who must hold it in every jurisdiction. The relevant legitimate human authority depends on the affected scope.
