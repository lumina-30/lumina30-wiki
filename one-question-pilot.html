Definition

The One-Question Pilot is a lightweight way to insert the LUMINA-30 boundary question into an existing review without replacing the organization’s current governance process.

General concept vs. LUMINA-30 usage

This is not a certification checklist or a complete AI risk assessment. It is a pilot question that surfaces whether effective refusal still exists before irreversible escalation.

Why this distinction matters

A compact question is easier to test in procurement, audit, deployment review, incident analysis, or a risk register than a wholesale governance redesign.

Limits and unknowns

A recorded answer is not proof by itself. Claims about effective refusal require traceable evidence, and an unknown or unverifiable answer should remain visible rather than being guessed away.
