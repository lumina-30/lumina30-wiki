Definition

LUMINA-30 is a non-binding public boundary framework for examining whether effective human refusal authority remains available before irreversible AI-related consequences occur.

General concept vs. LUMINA-30 usage

AI governance is the broad field of institutions, rules, practices, and accountability around AI. LUMINA-30 is a specific framework within that wider field. It does not claim to be a law, certification, safety guarantee, adopted standard, or substitute for technical safety work.

Why this distinction matters

The framework narrows a large governance debate to a boundary question: before consequences become practically irreversible, can a legitimate human still refuse, stop, suspend, verify, challenge, or redirect the process?
