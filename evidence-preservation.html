Definition

Evidence preservation means retaining the facts, logs, approvals, authority records, timing, and refusal-pathway evidence needed to test whether human refusal existed and could take effect before irreversible consequences.

General concept vs. LUMINA-30 usage

Audit logging is a broad technical and compliance practice. The LUMINA-30 question gives it a focused evidentiary purpose: reconstructing whether refusal was available, informed, timely, authorized, and operationally effective.

Why this distinction matters

A later claim that “a human was in control” is weak if the decision trail, permissions, system state, timestamps, stop attempts, or consequences cannot be verified.

Limits and unknowns

The official public material defines review requirements, not a universal retention schedule or chain-of-custody standard. Applicable legal and forensic requirements remain separate.
