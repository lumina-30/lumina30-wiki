Definition

A pre-incident boundary review checks whether effective human intervention remains possible before release, deployment, capability exposure, infrastructure connection, irreversible publication, or authority handoff.

General concept vs. LUMINA-30 usage

Conventional pre-deployment review may cover performance, security, compliance, or model risk. The LUMINA-30 layer adds a specific boundary check about refusal effectiveness before irreversibility.

Why this distinction matters

The review can expose a gap while the process is still stoppable: unclear authority, insufficient evidence, excessive speed, inaccessible logs, uncontrolled delegation, or a stop that cannot take effect in time.

Limits and unknowns

It supplements rather than replaces domain-specific safety, security, legal, ethics, procurement, or operational review.
