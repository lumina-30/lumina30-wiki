How should AI incidents be reviewed?

DefinitionGeneral vs. LUMINA-30Why it mattersLimitsPrimary sources

Definition

In the LUMINA-30 incident-review layer, an AI incident is examined by asking whether legitimate human refusal could actually take effect before irreversible real-world impact occurred.

General concept vs. LUMINA-30 usage

General incident review reconstructs events, causes, controls, and consequences. LUMINA-30 adds a refusal-pathway analysis: who could act, what they knew, how much time they had, and when refusal became ineffective.

Why this distinction matters

The official route begins by preserving facts, logs, approval records, and refusal-pathway evidence, then applies irreversibility criteria and treats absent or unverifiable evidence explicitly.

Limits and unknowns

This lens does not replace forensic, legal, safety-engineering, security, or regulator-led investigation, and it does not determine liability.
