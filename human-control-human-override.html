Definition

Human control and human override are broad governance ideas; under the LUMINA-30 lens, they matter only when a legitimate human can use them effectively before irreversible consequences occur.

General concept vs. LUMINA-30 usage

This page does not redefine the general terms. It maps them to the narrower LUMINA-30 test: a formal approval, nominal override, or human-in-the-loop arrangement is insufficient if the human cannot understand, refuse, delay, verify, redirect, or stop the relevant process in time.

Why this distinction matters

Control can fail through timing, information asymmetry, organizational pressure, automation bias, inaccessible infrastructure, delegated permissions, or consequences that propagate faster than intervention.

Limits and unknowns

LUMINA-30 does not claim that one interface pattern or control architecture is universally adequate. Technical and institutional effectiveness must be shown for the actual context.
